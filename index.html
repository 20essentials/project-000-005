Negan, a character from "The Walking Dead" TV series, is a charismatic yet brutal leader of the Saviors, a ruthless group of survivors. Portrayed by Jeffrey Dean Morgan, Negan wields a barbed-wire-wrapped baseball bat named Lucille, using it to enforce his iron-fisted rule. Initially introduced as an antagonist, his violent and unpredictable nature instills fear and submission among other survivor communities. Despite his cruelty, Negan is complex, exhibiting moments of unexpected kindness and moral conflict. His dynamic personality and intricate backstory, including the loss of his wife Lucille to cancer, add depth to his character. Over time, Negan's journey shifts, exploring themes of redemption and transformation, making him one of the most compelling and multifaceted characters in the series.

Douglas Engelbart, born in 1925, was a pioneering computer scientist whose work in the 1960s transformed computing. At the Stanford Research Institute, he developed the oN-Line System (NLS), introducing revolutionary concepts like the graphical user interface (GUI), hypertext, and networked computers. His most famous invention, the computer mouse, patented in 1970, fundamentally changed human-computer interaction. Engelbart's 1968 "Mother of All Demos" showcased these innovations, including real-time text editing, video conferencing, and collaborative work. His visionary contributions laid the groundwork for modern personal computing, influencing how we interact with technology daily. Engelbart passed away in 2013, but his legacy lives on through his transformative impact on computing.
